TOPIC 01 — STAGE 1

Angles & Measurement

Before calculating a single sine or cosine, you need to understand what an angle is and how to measure it in two systems — degrees and radians.

An angle is formed when two rays share a vertex. Positive angles = counter-clockwise. Negative = clockwise.

Degree (°)
Full circle = 360 equal parts. Intuitive — most people think in degrees naturally.
Radian (rad)
Angle formed when arc length = radius. Full circle = 2π rad. Calculus requires radians — the natural unit of mathematics.
Conversion
Degrees → Radians: × (π/180)
Radians → Degrees: × (180/π)
30°=π/6 | 45°=π/4 | 60°=π/3 | 90°=π/2 | 180°=π | 360°=2π
🔑
Memorize key angles in BOTH systems. 30°=π/6, 45°=π/4, 60°=π/3, 90°=π/2 appear in virtually every trig problem. The formula is your backup, not your first choice.

The Unit Circle — Heart of Everything

The unit circle is the single most important diagram in all of trigonometry. Truly understand it — not just memorize it — and you can derive almost any trig value on demand, without a calculator.

The unit circle: radius = 1, centered at the origin (0,0). Every point is (x, y). The key insight: for angle θ drawn from the positive x-axis, the coordinates where it hits the circle ARE cosθ and sinθ.

The Core Connection
Point at angle θ = (cosθ, sinθ)
x-coordinate = cosθ | y-coordinate = sinθ
Equation of unit circle: x² + y² = 1
💡
WHY is x=cosθ and y=sinθ? Draw a right triangle inside the unit circle. Hypotenuse=radius=1. Horizontal leg=x, vertical leg=y. By SOH CAH TOA: sinθ=opposite/hypotenuse=y/1=y. cosθ=adjacent/hypotenuse=x/1=x. The unit circle makes hypotenuse=1, so sin and cos directly equal the coordinates!
🧠
Only 3 values appear as coordinates on the entire circle: 1/2, √2/2 ≈ 0.707, and √3/2 ≈ 0.866. They come from two special triangles:

• 30-60-90 triangle → sides 1/2 and √3/2
• 45-45-90 triangle → both sides √2/2

All 16 points on the circle use these same values — only the ± signs change based on quadrant (ASTC).

Right Triangles & SOH CAH TOA

Before the unit circle, trig was born from right triangles. SOH CAH TOA encodes the three primary trig ratios. Master this and you can solve any right triangle problem.
SOH CAH TOA
SOH → sinθ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
CAH → cosθ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
TOA → tanθ = Opposite / Adjacent
🎯
The key insight: Trig functions are ratios — they describe the shape of a triangle for a given angle. sin(30°)=1/2 means "for every 2 units of hypotenuse, the opposite side is exactly 1 unit." No matter the size of the triangle, this ratio is always the same.

The Six Trig Functions

Trigonometry has not three but six functions. The three new ones are simply reciprocals of sin, cos, and tan.
Sine
sinθ = opp/hyp = y
Cosecant (1/sin)
cscθ = hyp/opp = 1/sinθ
Cosine
cosθ = adj/hyp = x
Secant (1/cos)
secθ = hyp/adj = 1/cosθ
Tangent
tanθ = opp/adj = sinθ/cosθ
Cotangent (1/tan)
cotθ = adj/opp = cosθ/sinθ
🧩
Reciprocal pairs: sin↔csc, cos↔sec, tan↔cot. Each pair multiplies to 1: sinθ×cscθ=1 always. When you see csc, sec, or cot, your first move is usually to convert to sin/cos/tan.

TOPIC 09 — STAGE 3

Graphs of sin, cos, and tan

Sine and cosine produce smooth repeating waves. Tangent produces a completely different shape with vertical asymptotes. These are the most important curves in mathematics.
Key Facts
y=sinx: 0→1 at π/2→0 at π→−1 at 3π/2→0 at 2π | Period=2π | Range=[−1,1]
y=cosx: 1→0 at π/2→−1 at π→0 at 3π/2→1 at 2π | Period=2π | Range=[−1,1]
y=tanx: Period=π | Range=(−∞,+∞) | Asymptotes at x=π/2+nπ
cos(x)=sin(x+π/2) — cosine is just a shifted sine!
